Richard Jones

Richard is an experienced software engineer who works comfortably on both back-end data management systems, and front-end visualisations.

Richard started his career in 2001 in academic libraries as a software developer, working on digital repositories and research information; he started at the University of Edinburgh, and later moved to the University of Bergen and then Imperial College London. He moved out to the commercial sector in 2007, taking a position at Hewlett-Packard's research laboratories, then at Symplectic Ltd before founding Cottage Labs in 2011.

He is a long-term contributor to open source, committing to a number of shared code bases including DSpace, and has published numerous utilities and applications. He has also contributed to a number of open standards including OAI-ORE, ResourceSync and SWORD, the latter of which he has stood as Technical Lead since 2011 and across two major versions.

He has written extensively throughout his career, including a number of journal articles, a book on Institutional Repositories, and a regular technical blog. Not to mention more project proposals and reports than he can remember.

At Cottage Labs he's responsible for a lot of business operations, customer relations, and business development. He's also an active and experienced software engineer, working on back-end data management systems and front-end visualisations.
