Jobs at Cottage Labs

Cottage Labs is a partnership of data experts who help our clients understand and utilise their data more effectively. We build tailored solutions for their needs, from software to processes, across the whole data spectrum. Our customers are primarily in academia and research, involving work with publications and research data. Projects run from a few months to years and usually involve development, customisation, and deployment of data management and analysis platforms.

Software Engineer - Python / Ruby on Rails / Node.js

The role

CL requires an experienced programmer to help us with our ever growing software development workload. Key responsibilities include:

  • Contributing high quality code, tests, and documentation to CL projects
  • Collaborating with project team members - discussing approaches to new features, reviewing pull requests, pair programming where applicable etc.
  • Communicating effectively with clients - attending meetings where possible, engaging with queries and technical questions relating to projects
  • Keeping up-to-date with best practice and promising new technologies

In addition, the role requires configuration and maintenance of your own development environment, and access to a suitable work environment. Cottage Labs does not have offices; all members work remotely.

This is a UK based remote working role. Proximity to the rest of the team in London, Milton Keynes or Edinburgh would be preferred for regular online calls and occasional in-person meetings.

Our stack

Cottage Labs has a variety of long-term projects with common components. We work in a wide variety of languages as clients and situations demand - skills in all of these languages and technologies are not required but any extra capabilities you can bring would be a bonus.

Essential

  • Proficiency in Python, Ruby on Rails, or Node.js
  • HTML & CSS
  • Some experience with JavaScript
  • Knowledge of version control

Desirable

  • One or more of: Flask, Django, FastAPI, React
  • ElasticSearch or Solr
  • Git and GitHub
  • Java, PHP, SQL

Demonstrated experience in digital library applications or related application domains is strongly desired. The role would require the ability to define and solve logical problems for highly technical applications.

In addition to development, it may be necessary to perform deployments and diagnosis of running systems, for which familiarity with Linux servers, remote access via SSH, and wider knowledge of web hosting would be useful.

Benefits

  • Remote working as standard (we have daily stand-ups and weekly team socials to maintain contact)
  • Flexible working hours
  • £35k - £45k depending on experience
  • Holiday allowance of 32 days including Bank Holidays
