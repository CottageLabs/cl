Jisc Monitor UK

Monitor UK is a service provided by Jisc to UK education and research for aggregating and analysing Open Access publication and its associated costs. Institutions contribute data either directly by spreadsheet or API, or using the sister service Monitor Local. Graphical reports are then provided for the stakeholders in the sector.

The reports rely on the presence of compliance information which may not always be present in the data provided by organisations. Monitor UK can connect to the organisation's Lantern account directly and enhance their records in the aggregation, improving the quality of the reports. This enables, for example, reporting on the distribution of spend between Hybrid and Pure OA publishers, which is of interest to many funders.

In the long term, this integration with Lantern will ensure higher quality reporting for the sector, and the opportunity for improved strategic decision making.

There are several ways you can use Lantern

By Uploading a Spreadsheet

If you want to upload spreadsheets from your computer, you should use our file upload option on the front page. Just export a CSV from your favourite spreadsheet program, such as Excel, and then upload it to be processed.

The content of your CSVs will need to look something like this, with up to 3 columns, one for each article identifier type we accept:

DOI PMID PMCID
10.abc/123 78543277  
10.jkl/8999/1    
  78024443 PMC75220165
... ... ...

Once you've uploaded it, you'll get an email with a link for you to check our process with. Then when we're done (which could take a few hours if you've given us a lot of rows) we'll email you with a link to download the results. Full details of the content of the results can be found in our documentation.

Via our API

For organisations who want to see Lantern integrated into existing systems, such as your repository, CRIS or other publications or compliance management system, we offer a fully-featured API option. With it you are able to:

  • Send batches of article identifiers for processing
  • Monitor existing jobs that you created
  • Download partial or complete result sets
  • Check information about your user account, such as your quota

The API also gives you more granular access to the data that Lantern gathers than the spreadsheet upload does. Full information about it is available in our API documentation

How does it work?

Lantern takes a list of article identifiers (DOI, PMID and PMCID are supported), via a CSV upload, submission via the API, or from an integrated third-party such as Monitor UK and Monitor Local.

Each identifier supplied is used to query multiple websites and data sources in order to locate information relevant to the item's Open Access status; for example, the re-use licence, whether it has been archived in an open repository, what embargo policies the journal has in place, etc.

Once all identifiers have been queried, the results can be retrieved as a CSV, via the API, or imported back into an integrated third-party.

Lantern aggregates data from a variety of sources

Where What you get from it
CORE Core aggregates items from digital repositories all over the world, so here we can find out whether your items are archived somewhere.
CrossRef CrossRef is a canonical source of bibliographic data, provided directly by publishers, and holds key metadata and in some cases rights information.
Direct from the publisher's website Where information is not available in the usual databases, we go direct to the article on the publisher's website to extract as much information as possible.
DOAJ DOAJ is the premier registry of Pure OA journals in the world, so we can use it to learn about the journals in which your researchers are publishing.
Europe PMC EuropePMC aggregates open and closed access bibliographic metadata for the life-sciences, and holds full-texts for OA publications that we can mine for useful information, especially re-use licences.
Grist Grist, operated by EuropePMC, aggregates information about grants, P.I.s and publications associated with life-sciences funders.
Sherpa Romeo Sherpa Romeo is a key resource for information on journal embargo and self-archiving policies.

In addition to these external services, we are constantly looking for new sources that could enhance your results, either through increased confidence in existing fields or the addition of new ones. We also monitor existing services for any new data they publish which we can take advantage of.

As Lantern is a labour-saving device which pulls information from a variety of sources for you, just remember that some of these sources assert re-use rights over the data we gather. We strive to only include sources with permissive terms, and you should check our sources documentation for more information.
